THE BASICS

WHAT IS CUTTER?

Our bookkeeper. It tracks every bit of work the crew puts in — farming, funding, cooking, selling — and pays everyone right on payday. No jobs to open or close — the operation never stops, and neither do the books.

  • Log work as you do it, in the stash-house channel you're working in
  • Your tab builds all cycle — work paid at its value, profit split by rank on top
  • Handoffs are safe: credit follows whoever did the work, not whoever's carrying it
  • Everything's logged, nothing's hidden — if a number looks off, the ledger shows who and when

THE SPLIT

TWO WAYS YOU GET PAID

①Work — paid at its value

Capital back — cash & bought supplies you fronted, in full. Farm pay — the value of what you farmed. Cook pay — a set rate per unit processed. Commission — a cut of every sale you make. Whatever your rank.

②Rank cut of the fund

After all work is paid, the profit left is the fund — split purely by rank (5/4/3/2/1) among everyone who contributed this cycle. You've got to be in the cycle to share it.

The books run in cycles. Your tab builds as you work; an officer's /payout is payday — everyone's tab + rank share, then a fresh cycle. Inventory stays put; only the tally resets.

CREDIT & CATALOG

🤝 CREDIT FOLLOWS THE DOER

Banking poppy seed someone handed you at the border? credit:@who pays the farmer, not the carrier:

/deposit item:Poppy seed qty:600 credit:@Vinny
/process line:Honey step:dry made:480 credit:@Tony
/sale product:Honey qty:1700 cash:220000 by:@Rico
🛒 EVERYTHING AT CATALOG

No price to type on /buy — Cutter values it at the catalog and you get that back off the top. Snagged a deal or overpaid on your own? That's yours — the crew reimburses catalog, no more, no less.

/buy item:Vial qty:200  → capital $10,000
/fund-cash amount:25000  → cash capital

Farmed materials are auto-priced from the product so the crew always keeps its margin — officers tune it with /config.

TAKING PRODUCT OUT

CHECKOUT, SELL, RETURN

Product leaves the shelf in someone's hands before it's sold. Checking out isn't taking it for yourself — it's crew product in your custody, and it doesn't touch your pay.

Rico/checkout product:Honey qty:200holding 200 for the crew
Rico/sale product:Honey qty:150 cash:18000sells from his holding first
Rico/return product:Honey qty:5050 back on the shelf — squared ✓
  • It has to square: out = sold + returned — /holding shows what anyone still has out
  • Got jacked on the run? Log a /loss — it can come off your holding, not just a house

WHEN IT GOES WRONG

BUSTED OR ROBBED

A house gets raided, you get jacked leaving a deal, product spoils. Log it the second it happens so the books stay honest:

Anyone/loss cause:busted item:Honey qty:200off the shelf, on the record
Anyone/loss cause:robbed cash:50000 note:hit leaving the dealcash gone, logged
  • Crew-shared by default — it comes out of the profit, everyone's cut takes a small hit
  • Clearly on one person? An officer adds charge:@member — it comes off their cut, not the crew's
  • Nobody ever ends up owing — a catastrophic loss spreads what's left, no one goes negative
  • Got it back? An officer /voids the loss and the stock's restored

WHEN THINGS GO SIDEWAYS

FIXES & HELP

  • Wrong number? Officers can /void any entry this cycle — it's logged, never deleted
  • Loss came back? /void the loss entry — stock and cash are restored

  • Every payday posts the full cycle record to #treasury — that's everyone's receipt
