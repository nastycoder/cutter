Why fixed values?

Catalog prices bake in farm labor & black-market cost. Whatever a member actually pays a player to skip the grind is their own deal — Cutter's math never moves. Built products are valued bottom-up from their inputs, never the street price.
FOUR WAYS TO GET PAID ① Capital home first — fronted materials & cash reimbursed before any profit.  ② Work share (70%) — farm, fund, or cook; paid on what you produce.  ③ Rank share (30%) — your level, auto from Discord.  ④ Selling commission — hazard pay for moving product.

TUNING IN ACTION
THE LABOR-RATE LEVER

At $25 / unit — capital leads

Fronting $50 buyables outweighs cooking. The money men land on top; Tony cooks 2,340 units and still finishes mid-pack. Right for a crew where capital is scarce and risky.

Toward $150 / unit — cooks climb

Push labor toward the cost of a buyable and the cooks rise to match the financiers. Lower it and farming & funding dominate. One number sets the whole balance.
HOW IT RUNS IN DISCORD /job open → /deposit materials & cash → /process each step → /sale logs real cash → /settle posts the payout table. /status reconciles an op and /ledger shows its full history — every entry on the record, so any split can be re-checked line by line.
THE BOTTOM LINE Front it, farm it, cook it, or move it — Cutter pays each the same fair way, scales the cut to rank, and keeps it clean as the crew grows. Set the dials once; the books take it from there.
